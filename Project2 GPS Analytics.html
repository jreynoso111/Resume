Project overview

Context

The company operates a large commercial fleet financed through long-term deals. Inventory moved between customers, lots, and repo yards faster than the systems could track it. NetSuite held the financial truth, PassTime stored GPS history, and multiple spreadsheets tracked titles, storage, and legal cases.

My goal was to create a single, daily-refreshed dashboard that reconciled those sources and answered a basic question with precision: “Where is each unit, what is its real status, and are we safe from operational and financial risk?”

  • Reconcile inventory snapshots (NetSuite) with GPS history and repo tickets.
  • Expose units with inconsistent status between systems ("with customer" but GPS at repo yard, etc.).
  • Provide operations, credit & legal with one place to review every VIN.

Solution

How the dashboard is structured

Inventory snapshot logic

Daily NetSuite exports are normalized and combined into a historic table per VIN, with calculated signatures to detect real changes (deal status, location, balance) without duplicating rows.

GPS movement status

PassTime history is summarized into “Last ping” and “Moving” flags, only if the last ping is recent enough. Older devices are marked as “Unknown” to avoid false confidence.

Exception-first views

Instead of showing all units, the main views rank VINs by issues: stale GPS, status mismatch, missing stock numbers, or repo tickets not aligned with accounting.
