Narrative

My Journey

My professional character was forged during 16 years of service at the Santo Domingo Metro. Moving back to New York has been a new chapter, blending my technical history with the fast-paced culture of NYC.

01
The Metro Era (16 Years)
I started at the Central Control as a Technical Assistant, but my curiosity quickly led me to the Maintenance Department, focusing on Telecommunications, Signaling, and Ticketing. Over 15 years, I gained deep technical training in railway security systems, installation operations, and quality control. It was here that I developed my passion for analytics, learning that data is the pulse of any operation.
02
Education & Growth
I hold a degree in Higher Technology in Food Engineering from the Universidad Autónoma de Santo Domingo (UASD). While my career pivoted toward transportation and data technology, that engineering background gave me a rigorous foundation in process management, safety standards, and project structure that I apply to everything I do today.
03
A Lifelong Learner
I thrive on challenges—from solving complex inventory problems to mastering new software tools. Moving back to NYC marks a new chapter, allowing me to bring my deep operational experience from the Dominican Republic to new, fast-paced environments.
