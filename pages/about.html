Juan Radhamés Reynoso Pérez is a maintenance and fleet operations leader focused on asset reliability, preventive maintenance, and inventory control.

His approach centers on "clean answers to messy problems," using data, analytics, and automation to support maintenance decisions, control costs, and keep critical assets available for service.

Biography & Education

Reynoso developed an early interest in how complex systems function. He pursued technical education at the Universidad Autónoma de Santo Domingo (UASD), where he completed coursework toward a Higher Technology degree in Food Engineering.

That training strengthened his discipline in:

•
Process control, quality assurance standards (ISO), and maintenance consistency.
•
Systematic analysis of inputs, outputs, and asset lifecycle behavior.
•
Operational discipline required for industrial maintenance and reliability environments.

Professional Career

The Metro Era (2008–2024)

For 16 years, Reynoso served in the Santo Domingo Metro, a critical transit infrastructure project. His tenure evolved from technical support into oversight of maintenance operations, coordinating reliability priorities, downtime response, and cross-team execution for complex systems.

01
Central Control
Began as a Technical Assistant, monitoring real-time operations, escalating maintenance-critical incidents, and supporting shift-level operational decisions.
02
Maintenance & Telecoms
Specialized in signaling systems, CCTV operations, and ticketing infrastructure, with emphasis on preventive maintenance scheduling, parts readiness, and fault prevention.
03
Crisis Management
Developed rapid response protocols to protect passenger safety, reduce downtime, control recovery costs, and sustain system reliability.

Reynoso cites this period as the origin of his "visibility first" approach: "When systems fail, it is rarely bad luck; it is usually a lack of visibility."
