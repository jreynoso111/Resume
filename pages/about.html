Narrative

My Journey

I spent 16 years in the Santo Domingo Metro, where reliability isn’t a slogan — it’s a duty. That experience taught me systems thinking: how to prevent failures, measure reality, and make decisions that hold up under pressure. Moving back to New York opened a new chapter: faster pace, higher stakes, and bigger data.

01

The Metro Era (16 Years)

I began in Central Control as a Technical Assistant and moved into Maintenance — Telecom, Signaling, and Ticketing. Over time, I learned something that still guides me: when systems fail, it’s rarely “bad luck” — it’s missing visibility. Data became my way to create visibility and prevent problems before they escalate.

02

Education & Growth

I completed all credits for a Higher Technology degree in Food Engineering at UASD. That training strengthened my approach to process, controls, and standards — the same discipline I now apply to inventory, fleet operations, and analytics.

03

A Practical Builder

Today I work with fleet and inventory data — cleaning, modeling, automating reporting, and building systems that make decisions faster and clearer. I like tools, but I respect fundamentals: accuracy, auditability, and results you can explain in one minute.
