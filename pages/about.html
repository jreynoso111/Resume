Juan Radhamés Reynoso Pérez is a data analyst and fleet operations specialist focused on practical performance improvement.

His approach centers on "clean answers to messy problems," translating operational complexity into clear priorities and measurable outcomes.

Biography & Education

Reynoso developed an early interest in how complex systems function. He pursued technical education at the Universidad Autónoma de Santo Domingo (UASD), where he completed coursework toward a Higher Technology degree in Food Engineering.

That training strengthened his discipline in:

•
Process control and quality assurance standards (ISO).
•
Systematic analysis of inputs and outputs.
•
Operational discipline required for industrial environments.

Professional Career

The Metro Era (2008–2024)

For 16 years, Reynoso served in the Santo Domingo Metro, a critical transit infrastructure project. His tenure spanned multiple disciplines, evolving from technical support to complex systems maintenance.

01
Central Control
Began as a Technical Assistant, monitoring real-time operations.
02
Maintenance & Telecoms
Specialized in signaling systems, CCTV operations, and ticketing infrastructure.
03
Crisis Management
Developed protocols for rapid problem identification to ensure passenger safety and system reliability.

Reynoso cites this period as the origin of his "visibility first" approach: "When systems fail, it is rarely bad luck; it is usually a lack of visibility."
